# NotebookLM Prompt

## Title

**"Edge Call: Gulfstream Park — September 6, 2014"**

## Prompt

Generate an audio broadcast in the style of a pre-race television analysis show. The format is a HOST and an ANALYST going race-by-race through a Gulfstream Park card, discussing where they see value and how they're structuring their bets.

The HOST is an informed interviewer — he knows racing, asks sharp questions, and pushes back when the analyst is uncertain. He sets up each race and transitions between segments.

The ANALYST has studied this card using a velocity-curve model that measures how fast each horse starts and how quickly they decelerate. He identifies pace dynamics, vulnerable favorites, and specific horses the crowd is underpricing. He explains exactly how to structure exotic bets (trifectas, exactas, pick 3s) to exploit the gaps.

Key themes to hit:
- They are only betting 3 of 12 races — discipline and selectivity is the edge, not action
- Race 1 has a vulnerable favorite with no measurable form data — the crowd is betting on faith
- Race 7 is the main event — 14 horses, $104K trifecta pool, and the model has strong opinions
- The pace dynamics in each race determine who benefits and who fades
- The bet structure matters as much as the horse selection — WHERE you place each horse in the trifecta determines whether you cash
- They caught the R1 trifecta for $95.75 on a $0.50 bet because they spread wide underneath
- They missed the R7 trifecta because they bet against the favorite who proved solid
- The horizontal (Pick 3) died on a 64/1 bomb in a maiden race with no data — highlighting the challenge of races without form

The tone is confident, direct, strategic. Two people who respect each other's knowledge, occasionally disagree on emphasis, and always connect analysis to specific dollar amounts and ticket structures. This is NOT a tip sheet — it's two professionals explaining their process and being honest about what worked and what didn't.

End with lessons learned: when to trust the model vs when to respect the market, and why spreading wide in the underneath positions of trifectas is the key adjustment that produced the day's winner.
