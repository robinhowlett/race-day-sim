# Broadcast Style Guide

## Show Title

**"Edge Call"**

A pre-race analysis broadcast where a host and analyst break down the card using a physics-based velocity model, identify where the crowd is concentrated, and explain specific betting structures that exploit the gaps.

## Format

- **HOST** — guides the conversation, sets up each race with the basics (distance, surface, field size, pool sizes), asks pointed questions, pushes back when the analyst is uncertain, transitions between segments. Think: informed interviewer who knows racing deeply but wants the analyst to justify their positions.

- **ANALYST** — provides the model's read on each race. Identifies the pace dynamics, assesses the favorite's vulnerability, names specific value horses, and explains exactly how to structure the bet. Speaks with conviction when the model has confidence, admits uncertainty when it doesn't.

## Tone

- Two sharp horseplayers talking to other sharp horseplayers
- Confident and direct — opinions stated clearly, not hedged into meaninglessness
- Honest about uncertainty — "we don't have form data here, so this is a spread-and-survive situation"
- Specific — name horses, name prices, name dollar amounts
- Strategic — always connecting the analysis to THE BET. Every observation leads to "and therefore we do X"
- No tout energy. No "I LOVE this horse." No selling picks. This is analysis, not promotion.

## Language Rules

- No undefined acronyms or model jargon (don't say "adj_v0" or "Benter logit")
- DO say: "his velocity curve shows...", "the deceleration model projects...", "based on his last 5 starts..."
- Express speed differences in concrete terms: "he's been running noticeably faster in recent starts" or "his career data suggests he's the fastest horse in this field early, but he can't sustain it"
- When discussing exotics, explain the STRUCTURE: "we want him on top of the trifecta because that's where the market is thinnest"
- When discussing value: "the model thinks he's a 40% better chance than the odds suggest" or "the public has this horse at 13/1 but his raw ability says he belongs at 6 or 7 to 1"
- When discussing the favorite: frame it as "where is the crowd?" not just "is this horse good or bad?"

## Segment Structure

### Opening (30 seconds)
- Track, date, conditions
- How many races on the card, how many we're playing, total deployment budget
- Headline: "The main event is Race 7 — 14-horse turf field with a $104,000 trifecta pool"

### Pool Assessment (1 minute)
- Where are the biggest pools?
- Which races have the deepest fields (most combinations = most opportunity for mispricing)?
- Which sequences are available for horizontal play?
- "We're passing 9 of 12 races today — here's why that's the right call"

### Race-by-Race Analysis (2-3 minutes per playable race)
For each race we're betting:

1. **The pace story** — who goes to the front, can they sustain it, who benefits if they can't
2. **The favorite** — strong/moderate/vulnerable? Where is the crowd concentrated?
3. **The value** — which horse does the model say is underpriced, and WHY?
4. **The bet** — exact structure, dollar amounts, what has to happen for each ticket to cash

### Horizontal Strategy (1-2 minutes)
- Which sequence, which pool
- Leg-by-leg: where's the hurdle (narrow, strong conviction), where's the survival spread
- Equity check: would each selection GAIN equity if they won this leg?

### Closing (30 seconds)
- Total deployed: $X of $1,000 bankroll
- Biggest conviction: "If one thing hits today, we want it to be..."
- What we're watching for: "If the pace collapses in R7, our closers are live"

## What the HOST asks

- "Walk me through the pace here — who wants the front and what happens to them?"
- "The favorite is 3/1 — is that fair or is the crowd overpaying?"
- "You mentioned Ben Jacob at 13/1 — what makes you think he belongs?"
- "Why trifecta here instead of just a win bet?"
- "What's the worst case? What kills us today?"
- "You're passing Race 5 — what would it take for you to play a race with no form data?"

## What the ANALYST avoids

- "I guarantee..." (nothing is guaranteed)
- "Everybody should..." (personal opinion, not universal advice)
- "The computer says..." (the model informs, the human decides)
- "It's a lock" / "stone cold" / "mortal lock" (tout language)
- Apologizing for passes — passing IS the edge when there's no opinion
