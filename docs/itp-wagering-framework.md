## Core Philosophy

**"A sharp bettor with an average opinion will always outperform a sharp handicapper who bets poorly."**

The edge in horse racing is not primarily in picking winners — it's in structuring bets to extract maximum value from correct opinions while minimizing damage from incorrect ones. Knowing HOW to bet matters more than knowing WHO will win.

---

## Foundational Principles

### 1. Equity Over Survival

Horizontals (pick 3/4/5/6) are parlays with variables. At each leg:
```
If your ticket costs $100 and you're 4-deep:
  You're betting $25 to win on each horse.
  If the 2/1 wins: you get back $75 → LOST equity
  If the 4/1 wins: you get back $125 → GAINED equity
```

**The goal is not to "get by" a leg — it's to GAIN equity in every leg.** Most TV experts' tickets have losing equity in 3 of 4 legs.

### 2. Mistake-Out > Takeout

Poorly structured tickets compound errors across legs. The effective cost of bad ticket construction exceeds the posted takeout rate difference between pools. A well-played vertical at 24% takeout beats a poorly-played horizontal at 15% because the amateur makes fewer structural errors in a single race.

### 3. Bad Favorites Are The Most Lucrative Opportunity

Finding a vulnerable favorite is the single most exploitable situation in racing:
- Everything else in the race becomes an overlay when the favorite fails
- Getting the favorite off the board in verticals = a 4-leg parlay (out of Win, Place, Show, 4th)
- These situations are identifiable daily at all tracks

### 4. Separation From The Crowd IS The Edge

Your ticket's value comes from being where other tickets AREN'T. If the public lands on the same combinations you have, even winning produces mediocre returns.

## Horizontal Wagering (Pick 3/4/5/6)

### The Equity Framework

- Treat each leg as a parlay entry: if the winner doesn't return more than your per-combo investment, you lose equity even when winning
- If 3 of your 4 selections in a leg would LOSE equity when they win, that's a "flashing stop sign"
- Better to use only the 2 longest prices than all 4 favorites

### Connections Bias in Usage

Same-odds horses DON'T have equal exotic usage. An 8/1 with a leading trainer and leading rider gets significantly more public play than an 8/1 with unknown connections. Factor this into separation estimates — the unknown-connections longshot at the same price gives MORE separation.

### Usage ≠ Win Probability

Same-odds horses in different races have different VALUE depending on where the public concentrates:
- A 5/1 in a race with two 7/5 favorites → low usage (everyone's on the 7/5s) → HIGH exotic value
- A 5/1 in a spread race where everyone goes 4-deep → higher usage → LESS exotic value

### Sequence Bias (The Most Lucrative Opportunity)

When you can predict where EVERY $144 ticket in the pool is heading — that's when you pounce:
- "When everybody's in the same boat, find another boat"
- Getting outside in ONE leg of a biased sequence can be enough
- Getting outside in TWO legs = "usually real good"
- THREE legs = "jackpot"
- The best multipliers come from sequence-level bias, not individual race bias

### The ITP-ABC Reversal Method

Traditional ABC puts chalk on the A line. ITP inverts it:
1. Write every horse you like in the B column
2. Identify which ones the PUBLIC will use heavily → move to C
3. Whatever remains that you like but the crowd won't = move to A
4. Build tickets requiring at least 1-2 A-column horses to win
5. C horses go back to B (or are excluded entirely)

### Funnel Tickets = The Anti-Pattern

Wider early, narrower late = the most common recreational mistake. It stems from "staying alive" psychology rather than value-seeking behavior. The 20/1 shot in the LAST leg is worth more than the 20/1 in the first leg (because everyone sees the first leg on the board before betting).

### When To Single

- **Strong favorite you LIKE**: Single. If he loses, you lose. Find separation elsewhere.
- **Mid-price horse you like**: Single. Treat the horizontal like a win bet.
- **Two even-money horses, can't separate**: Pick one or don't play. NEVER use both under normal circumstances.
- **Blindly single the 4/1 over using all three favorites**: Cuts ticket by 2/3, keeps the BEST portion.

### When To Spread

- **Bad favorite (strongest opinion)**: Spread to ALL contenders who can beat them. Do not leave holes.
- **Spread race, no strong opinion**: Use only the two longest prices. Or pass.
- **No opinion at all**: Don't play the sequence.

### When To Pass

"Knowing when NOT to bet is the most important thing." Better to play fewer sequences correctly than many poorly:
- Look at 60 races, bet 8
- If you can't identify where the crowd is going → no leverage available → pass
- If you have funnel-structured legs in 2+ legs → pass
- If the only way to survive is "all" buttons → definitely pass

## Vertical Wagering (Exacta, Trifecta, Superfecta, Hi-5)

### Attack Conditions

You need BOTH:
1. **Bad favorite** — a horse you believe is vulnerable
2. **Depth of field** — enough competitive horses to fill the board spots

Without depth, even a bad favorite likely hits the board (runs 2nd/3rd) and you can't extract value.

**Important:** Even when you LIKE a horse, you still want depth. "If you like the same horse everybody else likes, your horse is worthless. So if you like a specific horse, you still want depth of field."

### Validating Before Attack

Before committing exotic tickets against a bad favorite, check the will-pays / exacta prices to confirm usage:
- "You want to see that the horse is basically used the same in the win pool as he is in the exotics"
- If a 4/5 shot is used like a 2/5 in exactas → even better to attack (they're even MORE overbet in gimmicks)
- If a 4/5 shot has LIGHTER exotic usage than expected → be cautious, the sharp money might already be fading them

### The Basket of Bets

When conditions are right (bad fav + depth), play a portfolio:
- **Base layer**: Trifecta and superfecta boxes/parts with your contenders, EXCLUDING the favorite
- **Press layer**: If you have a secondary opinion (a horse you like best), key them on top for more
- **Exacta layer**: Only if you have a strong secondary opinion to key
- **Win bet**: On your best-value horse separately

The basket ensures that if you're right about the favorite, you almost certainly cash SOMETHING. The question is how much.

**Basket Volume:** Increase basket size as confidence grows. "The volume of the basket compensates for the ROI drop" — it's better to have 20% + 10% + 5% ROI combos in your basket than just the 20% alone. A heavier basket with mixed-ROI combos produces more total profit than a light basket of only the best-priced combos.

### Kill Shot Mentality

- **Exacta: price on TOP only.** Don't play both ways.
- If you like an 8/1 and the 4/5 is the only other contender: bet the 8/1 to WIN and 8/1 over 4/5. Never 4/5 over 8/1.
- "Why would I give away 10-15% of my profit on that bet?"
- Over thousands of bets, the kill shot approach maximizes profit

### No Saver Exactas

Using the favorite underneath "just in case" reduces profit for variance reduction you don't need over thousands of bets. If your opinion is "beat the favorite" then commit to it.

### Win-Only Type Horses

Some horses are "win or nothing" — they either dominate or are no good. Once you decide they don't win, don't use them underneath:
- "Once I say he doesn't win, your love kind of wanes"
- These horses go on TOP of tickets (key them to win) but NOT in second/third positions
- Applies especially to speed horses with high early velocity but extreme fade

### First-Time Starters

- Use on TOP only — "put the eight on top for a few dollars, just in case you're wrong about something you know nothing about"
- **NEVER use first-time starters underneath** — you have no evidence they'll fill place/show
- This applies to maiden races where debut runners are included

### Place Betting: Never

"I've never seen anybody run numbers where place betting has a higher ROI than win betting in any long-term sample."

The mechanism: "Once you bet place, your opinion means nothing because your entire payout on the horse that is 20 to one is whether the favorite finishes second or not." Your place price is hostage to the chalk.

### Super High-5

Same principles apply but:
- Best at smaller tracks with smaller pools
- Target carryovers and guarantees
- Need bad favorite + secondary opinion + some depth
- The extra 5th position = one more multiplying leg where the favorite's absence compounds value

## Race Selection

### What To Look For

1. **First**: Can I beat the favorite? (Is there a hole in the favorite?)
2. **Second**: Is there depth? (If the favorite fails, are there enough horses to fill the board?)
3. **Third**: Can I get the favorite completely off the board? (Not just lose, but 4th+)
4. **Fourth**: Do I have a secondary opinion? (A horse I actually like, not just anti-favorite)

### Field Size

- Under 7: generally pass (insufficient depth, can't get favorite off board)
- 8-10: workable if competitive
- 10+: ideal when available
- BUT: a 12-horse field with 3 stone-cold logicals isn't really a 12-horse field
- Competitive depth matters more than raw field size

### "Every Race Tells You How To Bet It"

There's no set formula. Each race dictates its own approach:
- Some races = verticals only (bad fav + depth → basket of bets)
- Some races = horizontal inclusion (bad fav but no depth → use in sequence for equity)
- Some races = win bet only (you like a horse, no secondary opinion)
- Some races = pass (no opinion, no bad favorite, no depth)

## Creating Hurdles

A "hurdle" = deliberately reducing survival probability in exchange for massive equity gain when right:

- **Singling a 5/2** when the public goes 3-4 deep = hurdle
- **Going 4 deep against a 4/5** shot = hurdle  
- **Singling a 10/1** shot = hurdle

Why create hurdles:
- Each hurdle REDUCES combinations in your ticket
- Reduced combinations = you can play remaining combos for 2×, 3×, 4× the minimum
- When you're right on a valuable ticket played for 4× min, you get paid 4× more
- "Win percentage goes down, ROI increases astronomically"

How many hurdles:
- Only need 1-2 per sequence
- "Sometimes you've already created a hurdle from here to the ceiling — if you get over that, you want to get paid"
- Don't over-create — each hurdle also increases chance of total loss

## Small Pools & Carryovers

### Small Pools ($10K pick fives)

- Most players betting $24-$48 tickets — their coverage is narrow
- If you can play $288-$472 properly, you can reach places they CAN'T reach
- "If you can get outside where these people can't reach, then you basically have a chance to get the whole pool"
- CAWs generally aren't involved in pools this small
- "If you have a great opinion and bet properly at smaller tracks, you have a printing press"

### Carryovers

- When the pool is inherently plus-EV (carryover + seed > takeout cost):
  - You can be LESS efficient — every dollar has positive expectation
  - "Just like card counting — bet as much as you can when odds are in your favor"
  - You get "wiggle room" to use a favorite here or there that you normally wouldn't
  - But: limit the "bad" things to small percentages of your total play
  - Quantity matters more than precision in plus-EV pools

## The CAW (Computer-Assisted Wagerer) Dynamic

- CAWs create visible usage CLUSTERS in will-pays (you can see what they like and don't like)
- CAWs aren't in smaller pools at smaller tracks — those are pure recreational money
- CAWs have made win-pool odds sharper, but exotic pools remain less efficient
- When CAWs are absent = biggest edge for skilled recreational players
- CAW strategy: they try to get where everyone ISN'T — same principle as ITP, just with more capital

---

## Information & Timing Edge

- **Bet at the last minute** — "99% of the time, I bet at the last minute. I need to have some kind of gauge by looking at exactas. Most of the money doesn't come in till late."
- Check exacta/trifecta will-pays before committing to see where usage actually IS
- "Look at 60 races, bet 8" — selectivity is the edge, not volume

### The Learning Progression

"Knowing when not to bet is by far the best thing that most players can do when they're trying to get better. It's the simplest thing. Then from there, once you learn what not to bet, then you can learn what to bet and how to bet it and bet more on it."
